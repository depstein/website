I am an Assistant Professor in Informatics at the University of California, Irvine.

I study how personal tracking technology can acknowledge and account for the realities of everyday life. More people are tracking today than ever before, and as a result they bring more varied expertise and goals than many apps and devices are designed to accommodate. I examine how the design of tracking technology can be improved, often prototyping and evaluating new design ideas.

My research is generously supported by the National Science Foundation and internal UCI programs.

I completed my Ph.D. in Computer Science & Engineering from the University of Washington, where I was coadvised by James Fogarty and Sean Munson. I received my B.S. in Computer Science from the University of Virginia.

When I'm not working, I try to spend time outside by hiking or running. I also collect turtle figurines, plushies, and pictures.
