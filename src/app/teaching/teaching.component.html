I typically teach courses in frontend implementation, Human-Computer Interaction, Ubiquitous Computing, and Personal Informatics at the undergraduate and graduate level. I also mentor undergraduate students as independent studies and as honors theses, and ran our department seminar from 2020 to 2022.

IN4MATX 133, User Interface Software

Programming and development of frontend user interfaces, with a specific focus on modern web and mobile technology. Emphasizes development with modern frameworks, learning to use external APIs, and storing in databases. Fall 2025, Winter 2025, Winter 2022, Winter 2021, Fall 2019, Fall 2018.

IN4MATX 153, Design of Social and Interpersonal Technologies

Understanding the foundational technical and social principles which inform the design of social technologies such as social media, and articulating the benefits and drawbacks of these technologies. This class was titled as CSCW. Spring 2024.

IN4MATX 231, User Interface Design & Evaluation

An introduction to the practical skills of human-computer interaction (HCI), with an emphasis on user interface design and evaluation. Covers practical methods for user needs and task analysis as well as interface testing, and guidelines for good user interface design. Fall 2019.

IN4MATX 232, Research in HCI

An introduction to human-computer interaction (HCI) as an area of research, examining and discussing different styles of research contributions and how they manifest in sub-areas of scholarship. Focuses on reading and discussing classic and modern research literature in a variety of areas, promoting breadth of topic over depth. Winter 2025, Winter 2024, Spring 2023, Winter 2022.

IN4MATX 241, Introduction to Ubiquitous Computing

Understanding and interpreting the move of computing towards ubiquitous devices, including the "internet of things" and wearable devices. Emphasizes reading of historical and modern research literature and implementing or evaluating novel ubiquitous technology. Winter 2021, Winter 2019.

IN4MATX 251, Research in CSCW

Examining the field of Computer-Supported Cooperative Work and Social Computing. Emphasizes reading historical and modern research literature on topics around the design and people's experiences with social systems. Spring 2024.

IN4MATX 285, Interactive Technology Studio

Introduction to programming and development for students in the Masters in HCI and Design. Specifically promotes appreciation and understanding of typical development tasks, to support collaboration between UX and programming. Spring 2025.

IN4MATX 295, Personal Informatics

Personal informatics systems help people collect personally relevant information for the purpose of self-reflection and gaining self-knowledge. Emphasizes reading of historical and modern research literature and conducting a project relevant to the topic. Winter 2020.

IN4MATX 299

299's typically designate graduate-level independent study. However, I have also run more structured courses under that title. Spring 2020 (Moving Human Subjects Research Online, with Stacy Branham).
